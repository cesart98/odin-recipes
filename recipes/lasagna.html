Lasagna

Ingredients

For the meat sauce:

  • 2 teaspoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 pound ground beef chuck
  • 1/2 medium onion, diced (about 3/4 cup)
  • 1/2 large bell pepper, diced (about 3/4 cup)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (28oz) can tomato sauce
  • 3oz tomato paste
  • 1 (14oz) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh oregano, or 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsey, packed
  • 1 tablespoon italian seasoning
  • 1 pinch garlic powder and/or garlic salt
  • 1 tablespoons red or white wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon to 1/4 cup sugar (to taste, optional)
  • Salt

To assemble the lasagna:

  • 1/2 pound dry lasagna noodles (required 9 lasagna noodles - unbroken)
  • 15oz ricotta cheese
  • 1 1/2 pounds (24oz) mozzarella cheese, grated or sliced
  • 1/4 pound (4oz) freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Put a large pot of salted water (1 tbsp salt per 2 quarts of water) on the stovetop on high heat while the sauce is prepared.
  2. In a large skillet heat 2 teaspoons of olive oil on medium-high heat. Add the ground beef and cook until it is lightly browned on all sides.
  3. Add the diced bell pepper and onions to skillet and cook for 4-5 minutes or until the onions are translucent and the peppers have softened.
  4. Return the browned ground beed to the pan. Stire to combine, reduce the heat to low and cook for another 5 minutes.
  5. Transfer the beef mixture to a medium sized (3-4 quart) pot. Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, and tomato paste to the pot
  6. Add the parsley, oregano, and italian seasoning, adjusting the amounts to taste. Sprinkle with garlic powder and/or garlic salt, to taste.
  7. Sprinkle with red or white wine vinegar. Stir in sugar, a tablespoon at a time to taste (amount of sugar needed may vary depending on how acidic the tomatoes are.)
  8. Add salt to taste (note that parmesan will be added, which is salty)
  9. Bring the sauce to a simmer and lower the heat to maintain a low simmmer. Cook for 15-45 miuntes, stirring often.
  10. Scrape the bottom of the pot every so often so nothing sticks to the bottom and scorches.
  11. Remove from heat.
  12. Heat the oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit.
  13. In a 9x13in casserole or lasagna dish, ladle a cup of sauce and spread it over the bottom of the dish. Arrange one layer of lasagna noodles lengthwise (about 3 long noodles, edges may overlap depending on your pan) over the sauce. Ladle a third of the remaining sauce over the noodles.
  14. Sprinkle a layer of a third of the grated mozzarella on top of the lasagna sauce. Add half of the ricotta cheese by placing cheese dollops every couple inches. Sprinkle half of the grated parmesan cheese evenly over the top of the ricotta cheese.
  15. Apply second layer of noodles. Top it with half the remaining sauce. Add half of the remaining mozzarella, the ramining ricotta cheese, and the remaining parmesan.
  16. Finish with another layer of noodles. Spread the remaining sauce over the top layer of noodles and sprinkle with the remaining mozzarella cheese.
  17. Cover the lasagna pan with aluminum foil, tented slightly so it doesn't touch the noodles or sauce. Bake at 375 degrees Fahrenheit for 45 minutes. Uncover in the last 10 minutes if you'd like more of a crusty top or edges.
  18. Allow lasagna to cool at least 15 minutes before serving. Leftovers will keep for about 5 days. May be reheated in conventional oven or microwave.
